An exercise in type and layout that grew into something more, Juris Privati is a minibook about the history and the will of the people to govern themselves in times of crisis.

Ornate lettering for the cover harks back to the days of hand-crafted arts and hand-farmed foods, when the United States was still very much a growing and developing nation with all kinds of promise ahead of it. Throughout the minibook, an attempt is made to create a cohesive feeling of time and place by utilizing black and white imagery and antique typefaces.

The minimal presentation and digital production contrasts with the aged look of the photos and type, old against new, and echoes the subject matter in utilizing the past to prepare for the future.
